Half Adder & Full Adder

Adders are the foundation of all arithmetic operations inside a computer. From calculators to CPUs, binary addition is handled using simple logic circuits called Half Adders and Full Adders.

Half Adder

A Half Adder adds two single-bit binary inputs A and B. It produces two outputs:

  • Sum (S) – result of addition
  • Carry (C) – overflow to the next bit

Truth Table

ABSumCarry
0000
0110
1010
1101

A Half Adder uses an XOR gate to generate the Sum and an AND gate to generate the Carry.

Full Adder

A Full Adder improves on the Half Adder by including a third input called Carry-in (Cin). This allows addition of multi-bit binary numbers.

A Full Adder is built using two Half Adders and an OR gate. Multiple Full Adders connected together form binary adders used in processors.

Why Adders Matter

Every arithmetic operation inside a CPU eventually reduces to binary addition. Understanding Half Adders and Full Adders is essential for learning digital logic design, computer architecture, and electronics engineering.
